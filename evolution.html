Our Evolution over the years

The Evolution of HISP Nigeria has been a remarkable and eventful one. HISP Nigeria was established in 2006 by a group of Social Scientists, Information Technology, and Health Information technology professionals. HISP was set up to drive the paradigm shift in health data and information management. Moving it from a data collection for reporting to information for functional planning and management approach. HISP works to bring equity and evidence to the Nigerian health systems space. Providing technical support for the Federal Ministry of Health and all partners and standing at the vanguard of health information management innovations and solutions construction. HISP is active and visible member of the global HISP Network, we are the official academy partner for Nigeria and have the deepest expertise and experience in delivering Health Information Management Systems content in Nigeria.   1. A Revolution in Strengthening the Health Care System

    HISP was established in 2006 and has been working towards building capacity for new systems through the DHIS 2, and overall support of the FMoH and SMoH throughout the country. HISP NG signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with the FMoH, which warranted it to actively roll out the District Health Information software (DHIS2) as the official software for data harvesting, collation and processing of the HMIS Data sets.

  2. Partnership for Transforming Health Systems (PATHS2)

    It is an organization led by Abt Associates (a conglomeration of professional associates) and funded by UK aid from the Department for International Development. The project began in 2008 and worked in five states of Nigeria: Enugu, Jigawa, Kaduna, Kano, and Lagos.

  3. International Participation

    The concern of DHIS in Nigeria heightened with the involvement of the Nigeria HISP team in all international activities of the global HISP Community. There has been a HISP Nigeria Team on every global congregation right from the onset in Tanzania.

  4. Nurturing the Culture of Data Application

    HISP broke the ice in HMIS pathways with her numerous service products like technical advice and assistance for appropriate ICT soft and hardware for health information systems, technical advice and guidance for information needs assessment, performance assessment and programme evaluations; design, configuration, roll out and management of information systems as well as applying GIS systems for high quality surveillance and out-break response management.

  5. DHIS Installation Expansion

    The Local Action Committee Against aids is an organization of the FMoH which sole responsibility is to handle HIV AIDS matters at the Local Government Area Levels of each state in the country. HISP has done well installing DHIS software in 6 states for the use of LACAs in those States. This was later on to become the responsibility of for HISP to cover for the whole country.

  6. WHO Influencing Health programmes delivery in Nigeria

    The world health body contracted HISP Nigeria to conduct training of officers on DHIS2 in 2016 before the commencement of developing a mobile DHIS2 platform to integrate the e-project in selected states of the country by 2018.

  7. Management Sciences for Health (MSH)

    Many jobs were undertaken for the Management sciences for Health (MSH) in conjunction with the Global fund. This organization identified HISP Nigeria as the corporate body with proven capability to surmount the current demand for a strong surveillance, monitoring and evaluation system (SME) in the country.

  8. Health Information System Programme Retreat

    The Health Information System Programme-Nigeria team took out three days in an all staff retreat to soul search and appraise over her years of service to the country; to see whether the company is thrusting forward as it should or not and change the organization's modus operandi, technique, styles, or manner after having performed a lot but still moving at a snail pace amidst plenty of opportunities.

  9. Rebranding

    APIN rebranded and changed its name from AIDS Prevention Initiative in Nigeria to APIN Public Health Initiatives

  10. Implementing Partner

    APIN is a direct implementing partner to the U.S Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and now implements projects outside the HIV/AIDS thematic area and has diversified its funding base to include other donor organizations. Our scope of practice has broadened to address other current and emerging public health issues. We work closely with the Federal Government of Nigeria and relevant stakeholders to strengthen policy & governance, service delivery systems, infrastructure and workforce capacity for health.
